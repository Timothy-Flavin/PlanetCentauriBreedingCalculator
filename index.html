Planet Centauri Breeding calculator V0.6.0

This is a calculator to find the best possible breeding pairs in Planet Centauri given a list of monsters
The first monster is your target monster. This means that you put the stats you want in the '0' slot.

Name: The name of the monster you are breeding you can use this
To help you keep track of things or just ignore it

Rarity: Rarity is the color of the monster's name when hovered over.
There are 7 levels or rarity, white, green, purple, blue, yellow, orange,
and red. 1-7. A monster can only level in a given trait up to it's rarity.
For instance, a green Scorpion with Life Bonus 7 will only ever get to Life
Bonus 2 because it's rarity, green, is rarity 2. Even at level 70.
The calculator will be looking for the chances of creating a monster
with a rarity equal or greater than the rarity of the target monster
so if monster 0 has rarity 7 then only red/legendary monsters will be
searched for.

Shiny: 1 for true, 0 for false. A monster's shinyness determines
whether or not the monster will have a prefix such as 'rare' or
'legendary'. This will also give the monster the pallet swap.

Hp: The hp modifier on the monster, 1-50. if the monster '0', the
target monster has an hp > 0, then the calculator will look for a monster
with an hp modifier greater than or equal to the target monster

Atk: same as hp but attack

Sex: M or F only or the calculator will not know what is going on.
if you want your target to be a male or female, put that. otherwise
anything goes. Monsters need to be opposite sex to breed

Attributes: A monster can have up to 7 attributes. how high a given
monster can level in a given attribute is decided by the minimum of
it's attribute level and it's rarity. (See rarity above) In this
calculator you must make sure that you name attributes the same or
the calculator may think that two monsters hace different attributes
when they don't. This is because I want this calculator to continue
working when new attributes are added, and not all monsters can have
all attributes. for example Life Bonus 3 is not the same as lifebonus3
Spelling matters and this calculator will think that 2 monsters with
life bonus written those 2 ways actually have different attributes.
if any of a target monster's attributes are left blank, The calculator
will assume you do not care what goes in those attribute slots.



If you have 4 hatcheries, this will find the combination
of 4 pairs which will give you the highest chance per
generation to get the target scorpion
